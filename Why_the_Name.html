We are in the process of rebranding from GlobalEyes to RunningChicken.org. We are in currently building a new website and planning a relaunch campaign. We wanted to move away from the more serious and cerebral branding of GlobalEyes to the lighter, more fun branding of RunningChicken.org that we feel is more in touch with the spirit of our work. The “running chicken” is inspired by a story from our team’s first visit to Kenya. This is more than a funny story though – the chicken represents wholesomeness, nutrition and sustainability. Livestock and animal husbandry is critically important to the livelihoods of the communities we work with and the projects that we collaborate to develop and implement.

The name GlobalEyes was inspired by the connection and subsequent partnership

that our organization was able to make with a Kenyan rural development group in 2006 -- globalization was the force that allowed us to establish and maintain this partnership. As we became more familiar with the issues and challenges that our partner faces, we realized that globalization was presenting both challenges and opportunities. So, we came up with “GlobalEyes” to convey our desire to leverage the opportunities of globalization while also working to mitigate negative effects that impact the communities we work with. We’ve tested this name for five years with mixed feedback. We’ve decided that the story behind the name is too complicated to describe and board members and folks not as looped into the above spiel have trouble delivering it. The negative connotation of globalization also

makes the name feel distasteful to some. Also, the challenges we hope to address are

serious, but GlobalEyes has started to feel too serious or heavy.
